Assignment 1 - Interactive Experience Research Project

Overview

    Title - ENDLESS SUMMER
    Author - Richard Mille

Notible Features
-Window interactivity
-Animated background

1) What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience? The first thing that stuck out to me was how the background colour shifted as the window resized even though it appeared to be a static image, so I was intrigued with how it was coded.

2) Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions. Clicking to turn on and off the music, and resizing the browser window to be taller and thinner as well as wider.

3) What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with? Changing the browser window to various shapes to see how the webpage changed, I also tried to see if it effected the backing music but I believe that it doesn’t.

4) What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience? Resizing the browser window, to various widths.

5) What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience? To be an interactive experience, showcasing colour changing use html, as well as shadow and light refractions. Potentially also using some form of Javascript to assist with this,

6) How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal? It has a simple text prompt that appears at the start as well as an arrow to the right-hand side of the browser window to show how to resize it.

7) What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times) I personally feel that it can really only be interacted from start to finish once as there is a clear start and end point, however during it I feel people can move it around as much as they so please or possible.

8) How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time? The arrow on the right-hand side moves as the window is scaled, as well as the sun rises giving a clear feedback loop for the user interaction.

9) What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference? I feel like its aesthetic match other works found in the synthwave art style, with the colour palette, sunset and palm tress being a common factor.

10) What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience? It doesn’t specifically communicate that as I believe it is more of an aesthetic reference.

11) What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience? The relaxed music and summer aesthetics lead to the feel of calm and warmth.

12) What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating? Sometimes the resizing is a bit laggy as well as I’m not sure how well it works on a laptop screen, it might be better on a larger screen. This is because there is only so much screen real estate that can be used, and I am confident it wouldn’t work on any other device that doesn’t support window resizing.

13) What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying? The slow and smooth shift of colour gradient of the sunset, this with the moving shadows and suns reflection on the water make a very immersive experience.
